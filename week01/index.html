Biography

I am Yu Xin Fang who is a lower junior studying emerging media at Hunter College. I was born on May 30, 2003, in Fuzhou, Fujian, China. In 2014, my family and I immigrated to the United States. I graduated from High School for Dual Language and Asian Studies in 2021 and attended Queens College. During my high school and time at Queens College years, I had no ideas about my major or career direction. At that time, I didn't know what I should learn or what I should do in the future. I had tried many different courses like early childhood education, nutrition, and corporate finance, but I chose media studies as my major. I ended up choosing media studies because of the inspiration from a co-worker when I worked at a milk tea shop. She is also a student at Hunter College, and I saw what she is doing with all those courses and found out I am also interested in things like Photoshop and filming. Therefore, I transferred to Hunter college and chose media studies as my major.

Outside of my academics, I worked and tried different part-time jobs such as a milk tea shop. Now I work at TESO Life, a Japanese fashion casual life product department store. I am trying all those different job that has nothing to do with my major just to enrich one's life experience and try to learn things from different perspectives from those works.

Artist Statement

As a media studies student, my work and creative practice are deeply rooted in an exploration of how media shapes our perceptions and influences our lives. My academic journey has been a quest to understand the intricate relationship between media, and technology, and to harness this understanding in innovative and meaningful ways.

By studying the evolution of the web and mastering current technologies like HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript, I aim to harness these tools to create compelling, media-rich websites that are both visually engaging and functionally adaptive. My projects are designed to respond seamlessly to diverse devices and screen sizes, demonstrating a commitment to inclusive and accessible design.
